Explore Rajasthan

Rajasthan, the largest state in India, is renowned for its royal heritage, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes ranging from the vast Thar Desert to the lush hills of Mount Abu. Known as the "Land of Kings," it is a treasure trove of majestic forts, palaces, and historic temples. Beyond its famous attractions, Rajasthan holds many unknown facts. It is home to Chand Baori, one of the world's largest stepwells, and the mysterious ghost village of Kuldhara near Jaisalmer. The state also boasts the Jaivana Cannon, the world's largest cannon on wheels, and Pushkar's Brahma Temple, the only one of its kind. With its unique art forms like blue pottery, Pichwai paintings, and a global highlight in the Pushkar Camel Fair, Rajasthan is a land where history, mystery, and culture intertwine seamlessly.

What's Famous in Rajasthan?

Blue Pottery

Blue pottery is a unique craft of Rajasthan, known for its vibrant blue hues and intricate floral designs. Made using a special mix of quartz, it is completely free of clay. Originating from Persian art, this pottery is used to create decorative items like vases, tiles, and plates, showcasing Rajasthan's artistic finesse.

Jodhpuri Juttis

Jodhpuri Juttis are traditional Rajasthani footwear, known for their intricate embroidery, vibrant colors, and durability. Handmade using leather and adorned with beads, threads, or mirror work, these juttis reflect Rajasthan's rich craftsmanship and are popular for both daily wear and festive occasions.

Metal Work

Rajasthan's metalwork showcases exceptional craftsmanship, with artisans creating intricate designs on brass, copper, and silver. From traditional utensils to decorative items like statues, mirrors, and jewelry boxes, the art reflects a blend of utility and beauty. Iconic pieces include engraved swords and ornate metal ornaments.
