Akshardham Temple

Akshardham Temple, located in Delhi, is a magnificent Hindu temple and spiritual-cultural complex dedicated to Swaminarayan. Inaugurated in 2005, it showcases traditional Indian architecture and is renowned for its intricate carvings, massive central monument, and peaceful surroundings.

Key highlights include:
▪️ The main temple, made of pink sandstone and white marble, featuring detailed sculptures of deities, flora, and fauna.
▪️ The Sahaj Anand water show, an immersive light and sound performance.
▪️ Exhibitions on Indian culture, history, and spirituality through films and robotics.
▪️ The Yagnapurush Kund, the world’s largest stepwell.

A visit offers a blend of spiritual enlightenment, art, and architecture.

HOW TO GET THERE

If you are traveling to Akshardham Temple by plane, you would land at Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), which is the main international airport in Delhi.

The nearest railway station is Hazrat Nizamuddin, about 7 km away. From there, take a taxi, auto-rickshaw, or metro to reach the temple.

Akshardham is located on NH 24 (Noida Mor), and ample parking is available at the temple.
Ride-hailing apps like Ola or Uber are convenient for reaching the temple from anywhere in Delhi.
