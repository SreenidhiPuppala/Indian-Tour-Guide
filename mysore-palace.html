Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical and architectural masterpiece located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. It served as the royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty, who ruled the Kingdom of Mysore for over six centuries. The palace is one of the most visited tourist attractions in India, admired for its grandeur, intricate craftsmanship, and cultural significance.

Key Features:

Architectural Style: The palace showcases the Indo-Saracenic architecture, blending Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic styles. Designed by British architect Henry Irwin, it was completed in 1912 after a fire destroyed the original wooden structure.

Magnificent Interiors: The palace interiors feature exquisite stained glass, intricately carved wooden doors, marble flooring, and beautiful frescoes. The Durbar Hall and Ambavilasa Hall are highlights with their elaborate gold-plated decorations.

Illumination: The palace is lit up with 100,000+ bulbs every Sunday, on public holidays, and during the Dussehra Festival, creating a breathtaking view.

Dussehra Festival: Mysore Palace is the centerpiece of the grand Mysore Dussehra celebrations, a 10-day festival featuring royal processions, cultural performances, and traditional rituals.

Museum: The palace also houses a museum displaying royal artifacts, paintings, costumes, jewelry, and weapons of the Wadiyar dynasty.

Why Visit Mysore Palace?

  • Experience the grandeur of royal architecture and history.
  • Admire one of the finest examples of Indo-Saracenic architecture.
  • Witness the magical illumination during Dussehra and special occasions.

HOW TO GET THERE

  • Nearest Airport: Mysore Airport (also known as Mandakalli Airport), located about 12 km from the palace. However, it has limited flights.
  • The nearest major airport is Bangalore Kempegowda International Airport, about 170 km away. From Bangalore, you can take a train, bus, or taxi to Mysore.
  • The Mysore Junction Railway Station is located just 2 km from Mysore Palace and is well-connected to major cities like Bangalore, Chennai, and Hyderabad.
  • From the railway station, you can take an auto-rickshaw, cab, or local bus to the palace.
  • From Bangalore: Mysore is approximately 145 km from Bangalore and can be reached via the Bangalore-Mysore Expressway in about 3-4 hours by car or bus.
  • Regular government and private buses operate between Mysore and major cities like Bangalore, Chennai, and Coimbatore. The Mysore Bus Stand is about 1 km from the palace.
