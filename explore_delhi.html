Explore Delhi

Delhi, the National Capital Territory (NCT) of India, includes New Delhi, the nation’s capital. Covering 1,484 km² along the Yamuna River, it borders Uttar Pradesh to the east and Haryana elsewhere. With a 2011 population of 16.8 million in the NCT and over 28 million in the National Capital Region (NCR), it is India’s largest metropolitan area and the second-largest globally.
Historically, Delhi was the capital of the Delhi Sultanate and Mughal Empire, with UNESCO sites like Qutub Minar, Humayun’s Tomb, and the Red Fort. It became the British Indian Empire’s capital in 1911 and saw a demographic shift after the 1947 Partition. The city is known for Sufism, Qawwali music, and poets like Mirza Ghalib.
Governed like a state, Delhi has its legislature, high court, and chief minister. Economically, it ranks fifth on India’s Human Development Index and second in GDP per capita. It has hosted major global events, including the 2010 Commonwealth Games and the 2023 G20 Summit, and remains central to India’s governance and development.

Best Time to Visit Delhi

Best Weather: October to March (Winter).
For Festivals: October to November (Diwali, Dussehra) and January (Republic Day).
For Budget Travel: April to June (Summer).
For Monsoon Beauty: July to September.

What's Famous in Delhi?

Indian Spice Box

Delhi houses Asia’s biggest wholesale spice market, boasting all type of spice known to man. Delhiites take fantastic pride in their spices and spices food, and also never go out without carrying some spices in their bag.

Embroidary Handbags

Delhi is a shopper’s joy. When you do decide to visit to Delhi, don’t forget to pick up embroidered designer clutches and also hand-bags. The craftsmen provide the Indian touch to these bags using genuine fine thread embroidery.

Madhubani Paintings

Delhi showcases vibrant Madhubani paintings at Dilli Haat and state emporiums, celebrating traditional art.

Meenakari Jewellary

Delhi's Meenakari and Kundan jewellery, famed for intricate designs, shine in markets like Chandni Chowk and Karol Bagh.
