Explore Kerela

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a tropical paradise famed for its backwaters, lush greenery, and serene beaches. Beyond its scenic beauty, Kerala boasts unique attractions like the Theyyam ritual art form, the world's only temple dedicated to Lord Parashurama, and the Kottayam district, renowned as India's first 100% literate town. The state is also a biodiversity hotspot with rare species like the Nilgiri Tahr and Malabar civet. Surprisingly, Kerala has the highest human development index in India and a matrilineal heritage in certain communities, offering a blend of nature, culture, and progressive values.

What's Famous in Kerela?

Kathakali Masks

Kathakali, an Indian classical dance form, is an important part of Kerala’s traditional art and cultural spirit. Through this dance form, dancers do the storytelling. They wear exotic costumes and face masks to portray characters from ancient legends and tales. These magnificent face masks are also available in Kerala’s popular markets. People buy these as souvenirs, decorate their homes with an authentic touch of Kerala’s history and charm.

Banana Chips

Kerala has very exotic dishes with many varieties of exotic banana chips with the best flavor of coconut oil. The chips are usually made from unripped bananas as well as tapioca, jack fruits potatoes and others. The trip to Kerala is not fulfilled without tasting and buying these banana chips.

Coir Handicrafts

Kerala is the best place to buy products made from coir. The state has been named after Coconut also called Kera. The state is home to coconut plantations. Every dishes in Kerala have coconut in some form. Many places that have coconut leaves are roofs of houses.

Kerela Spices

There are many spots in Kerala that have great spice plantations that are a must-buy, The best quality spices like cardamom, black pepper, cloves, cinnamon and turmeric are available in Kerala. You can also visit plantations and pluck the spices from there. The spices are available at economical prices.
