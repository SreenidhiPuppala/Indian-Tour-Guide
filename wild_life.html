Discover the Wild Side of India

Explore India's rich wildlife in its natural habitats, from Bengal Tigers to majestic Elephants.

Top Wildlife Reserves and National Parks of India

Kanha National Park

Kanha National Park, Known for its Bengal Tigers and dense forests, is located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is one of the largest and most renowned wildlife reserves in the country. Established in 1955, it spans over 940 square kilometers and is part of the larger Kanha Tiger Reserve. The park is famous for Royal Bengal tiger, leopards, wild dogs, and the barasingha, which was saved from near extinction here. It inspired Rudyard Kipling's The Jungle Book and is a UNESCO World Heritage candidate. Visitors can explore the park through guided jeep safaris and enjoy its rich biodiversity.

Gir National Park

Gir National Park in Gujarat is famous for Asiatic lions. It is also known as Sasan Gir , and wildlife sanctuary near Talala Gir in Gujarat , India. It was established in 1965. It is now considered the jewel of Gujarat's ecological resources.

Sunderbans Wildlife Sancuatry

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a vast mangrove forest spanning India and Bangladesh. Known for its Royal Bengal tigers, it's the largest mangrove ecosystem in the world and supports diverse wildlife, including crocodiles and rare dolphins.

Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park, located in Assam, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its population of the endangered one-horned rhinoceros. It also hosts tigers, elephants, wild buffaloes, and diverse bird species. The park features grasslands, wetlands, and forests, making it a biodiversity hotspot..

Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park, located in Uttarakhand, India, is the country's oldest national park, established in 1936 as Hailey National Park. It is part of the larger Corbett Tiger Reserve and is renowned for its Bengal tiger population. The park features diverse landscapes, including dense forests, grasslands, and rivers, supporting rich biodiversity. It is a popular destination for wildlife safaris and nature enthusiasts.

Ranthambore National Park

Ranthambore National Park, located in Rajasthan, India, is one of the country's premier wildlife reserves, famous for its Bengal tigers. It features dry deciduous forests, grasslands, and ancient ruins, including the Ranthambore Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The park is a prime destination for tiger safaris and conservation efforts..
